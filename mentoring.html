Resources

Here are some resources that I have gathered which can be helpful for econ grad students and to you as a mentor.

SMU specific

  • The Dr. Bob Smith Center offers free counseling services.
  • The ISSS has a page dedicated to getting a driver’s license, applying for a Social Security Number, taxes, etc.
  • Available SMU grants:
    • Dedman College Interdisciplinary Institute's "Graduate Student Summer Fellowship".
    • Moody School's "Provost’s Summer Research Grants". (Summer 2024 Call)

Econ Grad Advice

  • Chris Roth and David Schindler compiled an Econ Grad School Advice list.
  • Claes Bäckman has some resources for the “hidden curriculum” in economics.
  • Paul Niehaus has a great read for doing research as a Ph.D.
  • The podcast Hidden Curriculum has a ton of episodes with great advice:
    • Tips on prioritizing mental and physical health.
    • A story about finding a dissertation topic.

General Grad Advice

  • YouTube Videos
    • How to Deal with Burnout as a PhD Student - Coping with Stress
    • 15 Things I Wish I Knew Before Starting a PhD
    • The Powerful Way to take Notes as A PhD Student
    • TEDx Talk: Things about a PhD nobody told you about

Study Tools and Techniques

  • Ali Abdaal's Study Club playlist on YouTube is an awesome way to understand how to study effectively.
  • Notion : if you sign in with your student email you get educational perks!
  • Obsidian : you can learn more about it in this video.

Mentors

  • The AEA has compiled a reading list geared towards mentors.
  • The podcast Hidden Curriculum also have some episodes on mentoring.
    • A framework for mentoring
    • Tips on mentoring graduate students
    • Tips on mentoring undergraduate students
    • Understanding differences about the international graduate student experience
